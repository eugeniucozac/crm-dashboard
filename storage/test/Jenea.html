A.SCOPE OF WORK

A.1 Environmental Noise Survey

We would visit the site to undertake an environmental noise survey in order to assess the current noise profile of the area. The survey would be undertaken in accordance with ISO 1996-2:2007 Acoustics “Description, measurement and assessment of environmental noise - Part 2: Determination of environmental noise levels”.

The survey would involve automated noise monitoring over a minimum period of 24 hours. All acquired data would be used to render a representative snapshot of the local background noise during daytime and night-time. Further manual measurements would also be conducted in order to assess any variations in the monitored noise levels due to specific noise sources.

During the survey, an inspection of the surrounding area would also be undertaken in order to identify the location of the nearest noise-sensitive receivers relative to the proposed plant unit location.

The acoustic descriptors LAmax, LA10, LAeq and LA90 would be measured in order to provide a more detailed understanding of the acoustic identity of the site and set noise emissions criteria, in accordance with the planning requirements of the Local Authority.

A.2 Planning Compliance Assessment

A noise impact assessment of the plant installation would be produced based on the findings of the environmental noise survey and the available plant proposals and layout drawings.

The suitability of the plant installation would be determined and any mitigation measures, such as acoustic screening, would be determined to satisfy the noise emissions criterion. Detailed calculations will accompany the report in order to demonstrate planning compliance.

We would liaise with the Local Authority, should it be necessary, in order to agree on a noise emissions criterion based on the measured background noise levels. Our assessment would be based on the following Standards and Good Practice Documents:

•BS4142: 2014: ‘Method for rating industrial noise affecting mixed residential and industrial areas’

•BS8233: 2014: 'Sound Insulation and Noise Reduction for Buildings - Code of Practice'

•WHO Guidelines

If compliance to the noise emissions criterion cannot be achieved within the flexibility of practical mitigation measures, a redesign / reselection of the plant units may be recommended. Any additional assessment of alternative plant installation schemes are not included in this scope of work, and may be subject to additional fees, as appropriate.

B.PROFESSIONAL FEES

Our lump sum fees for undertaking the above scope of work would be £1,250.00 (+VAT). This fee includes the environmental noise survey, the data analysis, the planning compliance assessment and the generation of a technical report.

This fee is inclusive of all UK expenses, but exclusive of VAT, which would be charged at the current rate.

Payment should be made within a 30-day period after issue of our invoice which would be done after the completion of the environmental noise survey. Payment would be required to clear prior to the release of our technical report, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Please note that this scope of work reflects the time for survey and office work, commensurate to the type and size of the project. Any additional visits or meetings will be charged at our hourly rate of £120.00 per hour (+VAT). Additional site visits or meetings will only be undertaken with prior approval from yourselves.

The above fees include one (1 No.) revision of the report. Additional fees may be applied based on our hourly rate of £120.00 (+VAT), should further amendments be required.

Additional fees for commuting to and from the site may be applied as per our hourly rate of £120.00 (+VAT) in the instance of an abortive site visit, where circumstances are within the control of the Client.
